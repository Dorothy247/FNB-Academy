ELEPHANT

Elephants are large mammals land animals on Earth, known for their massive size, intelligence, and strong social bonds. They are easily recognized by their long trunks, large ears, and thick, grey skin. The trunk is a powerful and flexible extension of the upper lip and nose, used for breathing, smelling, touching, grasping, and making sounds. There are two main species of elephants: the African elephant, which is larger and has bigger ears shaped like the African continent, and the Asian elephant, which is slightly smaller with rounded ears. Elephants live in herds and are highly social creatures, often led by a matriarch

Elephants are herbivorous They are herbivores, feeding on grass, fruits, bark, and roots. These gentle giants are also known for their deep emotional connections—they can grieve lost companions, celebrate births, and communicate through low-frequency sounds that travel long distances. Sadly, elephants face threats from poaching, habitat loss, and human-wildlife conflict. Conservation efforts are crucial to protect these majestic animals for future generations.
